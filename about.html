Sheik Dawood, known as Chennai’s "Sandwich Don," started with a humble stall on TTK Road, selling just five sandwiches a day. Born in a small village near Madurai, Dawood moved to Chennai, where he initially worked in Koyembedu Market. These early jobs connected him to local vegetable suppliers, which later helped him start his sandwich business. Inspired by his brother Shahul, who saw potential in sandwiches, Dawood began selling his creations in a challenging market where idli, dosa, and chaat were preferred.

Initially, his stall struggled, and he had just ten menu items, with prices as low as Rs.6. Despite early financial setbacks and occasional confrontations with locals, Dawood’s dedication led to a steady customer base, with people drawn to his secret green chutney and creative recipes. As demand grew, he expanded the menu to 100+ varieties and launched the successful franchise brand Sandwich Square with his brother Jinnah and two partners.

Today, Royal Sandwich has 15 franchises across Chennai and outlets in Trichy and Vijayawada, with interest from international markets. Despite his success, Dawood remains humble, crediting patience and lessons from failures for his journey—proving that persistence and passion can transform small dreams into thriving empires.
